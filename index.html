1914
Born to Polish immigrants Daniel Salk and Dora Salk in New York on October 28, 1914.

1939
Salk earned his degree from the New York University of Medicine.

1947
Salk became head of the Virus Research Lab at the University of Pittsburgh, and began investigating the poliovirus.

1952
It was in the year 1952 that years of painstaking research, on the devastating virus, bore fruit as the initial tests were conducted.

1954
National testing of the vaccine began on one million children, ages 6-9.

1955
He was awarded the Congressional Gold Medal and the vaccine was proven safe and effective!

1962
Polio cases dropped to 910.

1963
Salk founded the Salk Institute for Biological Studies.

1995
This eminent virologist died at the age of 80 of a cardiac arrest on June 23, 1995.
